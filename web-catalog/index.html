Quick-Start Guide for Beginners

1 Choose Your First Project

Start with an Easy difficulty project in the $50–$100 profit range. Planter boxes and simple shelves are great first projects — they require basic tools and can be completed in a weekend.

Tip: Use the filters on the left to narrow down to Easy projects, then sort by “Time (Shortest)” to find the quickest wins.

2 Gather Tools & Materials

Essential starter tools: drill/driver, circular saw, tape measure, speed square, clamps, sandpaper. Budget about $200–$300 for basic tools if starting from scratch.

Buy materials from local lumber yards for better prices than big box stores. Check the Material Cost Estimator above for current pricing in your area.

3 Build & Finish

Follow the PDF plans included with each project. Take your time on your first build — quality matters more than speed when building your reputation.

Sand through grits (80 → 120 → 220) and apply a quality finish. Cedar projects look great with a natural oil finish; pine projects benefit from stain + polyurethane.

4 Price & Sell

Use the profit estimates in this catalog as a starting guide. Check the “Trending Now” section to see what is in demand in your area.

Best selling platforms: Facebook Marketplace (local, no fees), Kijiji, Etsy (online reach), local craft fairs, and word of mouth.

Take great photos in natural light and write detailed descriptions mentioning wood type, dimensions, and finish.

5 Scale Up

Once comfortable, batch-produce your best sellers. Building 5 of the same item is much faster per-unit than one-offs.

Use the comparison tool to find projects with the best hourly rate, and check seasonal trends to plan your production calendar.
